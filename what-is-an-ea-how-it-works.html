What is an EA (expert advisor) and how does it work?

If you've spent any time around forex or gold trading, you've probably heard people talk about “EAs”. But what exactly is an expert advisor, and how does it actually work? Here's a clear, beginner-friendly explanation — including what an EA can and can't do.

What is an EA?

EA stands for expert advisor. It's a small program that runs inside a trading platform — usually MetaTrader 4 (MT4) or MetaTrader 5 (MT5) — and trades automatically on your behalf. Instead of you watching charts and clicking buy or sell, the EA follows a set of pre-programmed rules and places trades for you.

Think of it as a trading strategy turned into software. Whatever a human trader might decide (“if these conditions are met, open a buy trade with this stop loss”), an EA can do the same thing — instantly, consistently, and without emotion.

How does an EA work?

An EA constantly reads live market data and checks it against its rules. When the conditions line up, it acts. The basic cycle looks like this:

  1. Watch the market — the EA monitors price, indicators, and other data tick by tick.
  2. Check the rules — it compares what's happening now to its entry conditions.
  3. Open a trade — if the conditions match, it places a buy or sell order, usually with a stop loss and take profit.
  4. Manage the trade — it can move stops, take partial profit, or close the position based on its logic.
  5. Repeat — it keeps doing this 24 hours a day while the market is open.

What an EA can — and can't — do

An EA is excellent at doing exactly what it's told, without hesitation or emotion. It never gets tired, greedy, or fearful, and it can react far faster than a human. That discipline is its biggest strength.

But an EA is not magic. It can't predict the future, and it only follows the rules it was given — so a flawed strategy will lose money automatically, just as a good one can trade well. It also can't adapt to conditions its creator never planned for, which is why monitoring and sensible risk settings still matter.

Key point: an EA is a tool that executes a strategy. It's only as good as the strategy behind it and the risk settings you give it — it is not a guaranteed money machine.

Bottom line

An expert advisor is simply a trading strategy turned into software that runs on MT4 or MT5 and trades for you automatically. Used with a solid strategy, realistic expectations, and proper risk management, it's a powerful tool. Used blindly, it's just a fast way to follow a bad plan. Understand what your EA does before you trust it with real money.
