Pips, points and dollars on gold — the quick reference

Gold confuses a lot of traders because brokers quote it differently. Here's the mapping this calculator uses:

UnitPrice moveValue per standard lot (100 oz)
1 pip$0.10$10
1 point / cent$0.01$1
$1 move$1.00$100

So 1 pip = 10 points, and a full $1 move in gold = 10 pips = 100 points. Scale any of these by your lot size to get the money value. At 0.10 lots, a 100-pip move ($10 of price) is worth $100.

Why this matters for an EA

If your EA's stop-loss is set in "points" but you think in "pips", you can accidentally risk 10× what you intended. Always confirm which unit your EA and broker use before going live — the difference is an order of magnitude.
